# Usage Guide

This skill helps a lawyer, compliance reviewer, or business team generate a short China PIPL notice for a registration or application form used in events, startup competitions, innovation awards, partnering days, project roadshows, or collaboration opportunity campaigns.

## How to Use

Invoke the skill with the facts of the activity and the form fields. Example:

> Use $pipl-event-privacy-notice to draft a short Chinese personal information notice for a startup competition application form. The organizer is [company]. The form collects name, job title, company name, city, email, phone, project name, project summary, therapeutic area, modality, and pitch deck. The data will be shared with [overseas HQ] for project evaluation. Rights contact: [email].

If the form is in a screenshot or Word/PDF template, first extract the visible activity name, field list, co-organizers, platform/vendor clues, and uploaded-material requirements. Then use the skill to decide whether the general template fits and what modules need editing.

## Minimum Facts to Provide

- activity name and type
- personal information handler
- exact form fields
- whether users upload project materials or BP
- service providers/co-organizers
- overseas recipients, if any
- rights contact email
- whether later marketing or similar event invitations will be sent

## Recommended Review Questions

Ask these before finalizing if the facts are missing:

- Who decides the purposes and means of processing?
- Is any overseas HQ, overseas affiliate, overseas reviewer, or overseas system involved?
- Are project materials expected to contain team member resumes, photos, email addresses, phone numbers, or founder information?
- Will the information be used only for the current activity, or also for later event invitations and cooperation opportunities?
- Does the business have a fixed retention rule, or should the notice use a retention determination method?

## Delivery Standard

Deliver a paste-ready notice, concise checkbox text, and a short checklist of covered legal items. Keep the notice readable for a one-page form and avoid cold legalistic language.
